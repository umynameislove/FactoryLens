# KI-012 - Label ink smudge from packaging contact
Defect type: print
Symptom: A soft-edged dark or colored mark sits on the shell surface and can look like a printed patch rather than physical damage. The shape may repeat across several pieces but with slight smearing.
Likely root cause: Wet label ink, transfer film, or packaging contact can mark shells during staging or manual handling. The issue is usually cosmetic unless the mark indicates contamination.
Recommended action / SOP: Check recent packaging material, label printer maintenance, and whether labeled trays or cartons touched exposed product. Isolate visibly marked units, wipe-test a sample if the SOP allows it, and keep packaging materials away from open product flow.
Severity: low

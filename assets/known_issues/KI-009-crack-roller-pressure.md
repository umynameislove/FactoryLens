# KI-009 - Roller pressure hairline crack
Defect type: crack
Symptom: A narrow hairline crack appears along the shell curve, usually with a slightly polished edge where the shell contacted a roller or belt. The line can be faint in normal light but becomes clearer on the heatmap or under side lighting.
Likely root cause: Roller pressure or belt tension may be too high for a brittle lot, especially after drying. The defect often shows up after a change in gap setting, speed, or upstream feed rate.
Recommended action / SOP: Check the latest roller gap and belt tension settings, compare the affected timestamp with maintenance or changeover notes, and inspect several nearby units from the same lot. If the pattern repeats, reduce pressure in small steps and re-sample before releasing the lot.
Severity: high
